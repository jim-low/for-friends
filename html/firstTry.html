Logarithms

Logarithms are the inverse functions for exponentials. They are also a way of looking differently at exponentials or indices.Take for example 25, think of 5(the exponent) as time and 2(the base) as the growth rate.So 25 = 32, or i can also say "If something is doubling(2) over the period of 5 times,the result will be 32". What if i say 20? Well 20 = 1 meaning that if i had an object that is doubling but over a period of 0 times, the result(1) holds the definition of no change because it was not growing over any period of time.Same goes to 2-1,in mathematics the negative sign is a simple way of saying "opposite".Therefore we can say 2-1 =
1 2
as "something that is doubling backwards over a period of 1 is equal to one half" because the opposite of doubling is dividing. Once you've grasped the concept here,let's head on over to learning about Logarithms.

Additional Information

  1. logab = c : the value of "a" must be > 0, the value of "b" ≠ 0. Basically, value of "a" must be positive and "b" cannot be equal to 0. Whereas "c" can be in any range.
  2. loga(ab) = b
  3. alogab = b
  4. logab =
    1 logba
  5. logab = logcb × logac
